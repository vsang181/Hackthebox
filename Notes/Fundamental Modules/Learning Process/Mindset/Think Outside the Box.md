# Think Outside the Box

Lets us start by asking yourself a simple question: what constraints were you actually given?

In this case, the answer is none.

So why did you not consider adding extra digits, changing the numbers, or replacing the arithmetic operations entirely?

This is where the so-called hacker mindset begins. It is commonly described as thinking outside the box, but in practice it means questioning assumptions that were never explicitly stated.

You likely followed an invisible set of rules without realising it. Those rules were not part of the task itself; they were habits formed by how you have been taught to approach problems in the past. Most people instinctively assume that the input, structure, or method shown in an example must remain unchanged, even when nothing says that it has to.

As you progress through this learning path, you will gradually pick up the knowledge and experience needed to spot these hidden assumptions more easily. That understanding will not appear all at once. It develops over time, through exposure to unfamiliar problems and repeated practice.

Before you can change how you think, you first need to understand how you currently approach problems. Pay attention to the limits you place on yourself. Ask whether those limits are real or simply inherited from habit.

This is the skill you are training here: recognising assumptions, challenging them, and deliberately exploring alternatives. That is what thinking outside the box really means, and it is a mindset you will continue to refine as you move forward.
