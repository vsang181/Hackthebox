## Practising on the Main Platform

[HTB Academy](https://academy.hackthebox.com/) delivers guided courses with structured content. The [main Hack The Box platform](https://www.hackthebox.com/) takes a challenge-based, self-guided approach to the same material. Using both together creates a more complete learning experience than either alone.

### Starting Point

[Starting Point](https://app.hackthebox.com/starting-point) has been redesigned to mirror the Academy style while keeping the self-guided approach intact. It is divided into three tiers of increasing difficulty:

- Tier 0: Entry-level machines
- Tier 1: Intermediate difficulty
- Tier 2: More complex, requiring chained skills

Each machine includes guided questions and a writeup for learners who need direction. A strong practice goal is to complete all currently published Starting Point machines.

### Tracks

[Tracks](https://app.hackthebox.com/tracks) are curated pathways built from challenges and machines focused on a specific topic or skill. Use tracks to directly target concepts identified as weak spots during the review step above. Completing one or more tracks aligned to your concept review list is a practical and focused way to improve.

### Pro Labs

[Pro Labs](https://www.hackthebox.com/hacker/pro-labs) are the closest simulation to a real enterprise penetration test that HTB offers. Each lab models a full enterprise-grade IT environment and provides minimal information upfront, requiring the same approach as a real engagement. Details for each lab are available on the respective overview page on the main platform.

## Giving Back

Helping others in the community reinforces your own understanding and develops soft skills at the same time. The [HTB Academy Discord](https://discord.gg/hackthebox) channels are a good starting point. Other ways to contribute include:

- Starting an educational YouTube channel
- Writing a technical blog with walkthroughs and non-technical posts
- Sharing short tips and tricks on social media
- Building a live learning community around skill development
- Assisting others with resume development and job searching

## Starting a Career in Penetration Testing

The skills built through this path are in high demand. Technical ability alone is not enough; the following soft skills are equally important for working as a penetration tester:

- Communication and presentation
- Teamwork and collaboration
- Writing, covering reports, documentation, email, and resumes
- Emotional intelligence
- Courage and good ethical habits

Practise explaining technical concepts to people outside the field. This kind of interaction directly prepares you for interviews and client-facing situations. If you are not already employed as a penetration tester, start applying now.

### Finding Work

- Attend industry conferences and meet hiring managers in person
- Contribute consistently and helpfully in infosec-focused Discord communities
- Search job boards including [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/), [Indeed](https://www.indeed.com/), and [ZipRecruiter](https://www.ziprecruiter.com/)
- Leverage word-of-mouth recommendations from people you have worked with or helped
- Use [HTB's Talent Search](https://www.hackthebox.com/business/talent-search) to connect directly with hiring organisations
